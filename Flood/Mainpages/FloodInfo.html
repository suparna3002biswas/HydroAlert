What is a Flood?

A flood is an overflow of water onto land that is normally dry. Floods can occur due to heavy rainfall, rapid snowmelt, or dam failure.

Why Do Floods Occur?

Floods can occur for various reasons, including:

  • Heavy rainfall
  • Rapid snowmelt
  • Storm surges from hurricanes
  • Dam or levee failure

Precautions to Take

To minimize the impact of floods, consider the following precautions:

  • Stay informed about weather conditions.
  • Have an emergency kit ready.
  • Know your evacuation routes.
  • Do not drive through flooded areas.

Flood Prone Areas in India

India is prone to flooding due to its diverse geography and climate. Some of the most flood-affected regions include:

  • Brahmaputra Valley: This region in Assam experiences severe flooding during the monsoon season due to heavy rainfall and the overflowing of the Brahmaputra River.
  • Ganges River Basin: States like Uttar Pradesh and Bihar often face floods from the Ganges River, especially during the monsoon.
  • Coastal Areas: States like Kerala and Tamil Nadu are vulnerable to flooding from storm surges and heavy rains, particularly during cyclones.
  • Western Ghats: The hilly regions of Maharashtra and Karnataka can experience landslides and flooding during heavy rains.
